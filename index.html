How the Story Is Played

These are works of interactive fiction: the book writes itself as you go, and it goes wherever you tell it. You play the part of Alice.

I — CommandSay what Alice does

Type plain instructions at the > prompt — look, take the key, go north, follow the rabbit. Type help in the game for the full art of conversation, and think whenever you are lost.

II — SaveDrop a bookmark

Type save at any moment and a little bookmark file falls straight into your Downloads folder. Keep as many as you please — this dream holds a hundred points across twelve chapters.

III — RestoreFind your way back

Returning later — even on another machine — type restore and hand the bookmark back. The curtain lifts, and you are behind the little door exactly where you left it.

The novels of Lewis Carroll (1865 & 1871) and the engravings of Sir John Tenniel are in the public domain; the illustrations here were digitised by Standard Ebooks.

The Wonderland game is written in Inform 6 and runs in your browser on the ZVM engine with GlkOte, by Dannii Willis and Andrew Plotkin. No account, no server: the story plays entirely on your side of the glass.
